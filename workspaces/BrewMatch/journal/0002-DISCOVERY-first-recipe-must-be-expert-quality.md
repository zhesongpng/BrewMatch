# DISCOVERY: First Recipe Must Be Expert-Quality, Not Personalized

Date: 2026-05-09
Phase: /analyze
Source: Value proposition critique (01-research/03-value-proposition-critique.md)

## Finding

The original brief frames personalization as the core value proposition: "the app gets better over time." This framing admits the app starts weak. The correct framing inverts this:

**The first recipe must be genuinely excellent on its own** — grounded in the curated knowledge base with bean-profile matching. Personalization is the retention mechanism, not the acquisition hook.

## Evidence

- Users compare first experience against a free Hoffmann YouTube recipe — if BrewMatch's first recipe is worse, they churn immediately
- 10+ brews are needed before personalization delivers measurable improvement — most users never reach this
- The "chronic not acute" pain means users have zero patience for a learning curve
- A good generic recipe (from the knowledge base) works for 80-90% of light-roast beans with zero modification

## How to Apply

- The RAG retrieval pipeline is the day-one value driver, not the personalization layer
- Recipe quality in the knowledge base is more important than the personalization algorithm
- The demo should show the first recipe working well, then show personalization making it even better
- Invert the pitch: "the best recipe for YOUR beans, right now" (knowledge base) + "and it keeps improving" (personalization)
