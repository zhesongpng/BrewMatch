Date: 2026-05-09
Phase: /analyze
Source: User correction during value audit

## Decision

BrewMatch is being evaluated and built as a real commercial product with a business case, target users, and a pricing model. It is NOT a course project or academic exercise.

## Why

The analysis agents inferred "MBA course project" from the folder path (MGMT655 - Machine Learning For Decision Making) and injected a grading/academic framing throughout the value audit and analysis. The user corrected this: the folder path is just where repos are stored. The brief describes a real product.

## What Changed

- The value audit was re-run evaluating BrewMatch as a commercial product (product-market fit, business viability, competitive positioning) rather than as an academic submission
- The brief maintains its business case (S$5-8/month freemium, Singapore market, specialty coffee segment)
- The product viability audit concluded: not viable as a S$5-8/month subscription due to zero willingness-to-pay evidence, retention cliff, and free alternatives covering 80% of the value
- The recommended pivot (diagnosis-first) is a product strategy decision, not an academic optimization

## How to Apply

- Never frame BrewMatch in terms of grading, course submission, or academic evaluation
- Evaluate all decisions in terms of user value, business viability, and market fit
- The brief is the authority on product vision; the folder path is irrelevant
